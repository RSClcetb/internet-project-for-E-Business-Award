Customer Relationship Management systems (CRM)

Microsoft Dynamics 365


Dynamics.microsoft.com
This CRM system by Microsoft scales much better for larger business, its focuse is sales, marketing and help desk functionality. They have both online and on premises solutions available.

The main point in it’s favour is it works seamlessly with Microsoft applications like the office suite. They feature a lot of predictive features like data on which customers are likey to convert and buy which it calls “contextual insights” aswell as offering a suite of productivity tools.

Microsoft seems to cover all bases with the exception of third party app support as it leans heavy on it’s users to instead make use of Microsoft apps.

Insightly


insightly.com
Insightly is a CRM aimed primarily at smaller businesses, it seems to excel in regards to sales teams.

  • Marketing
  • Sales, (quote generation, email tracking)
  • Project Management
  • App intergration (gmail, mailchimp, quickbooks etc.)

Payment Systems

These are systems that allow a business to process many forms of payment usually from a variety of currencies too.

Paypal


paypal.com
Paypal was one of the early successes when it comes to online monetary transactions. While it has a large consumer facing element it is used by a lot of smaller vendors to process payments through their online storefronts. They accept the following forms of payment.

  • Paypal Cash
  • From a Bank Account
  • Credit Cards
  • Debit Cards

Authorize.Net


authorize.net
Often referred to as the most popular payment gateway on the internet authorize.net has a lot of customers.

More business facing than Paypal, they specialise in payment gateways for ecommerce sites. As such many of us have used their service without really having been aware of it.

It is also widely supported with compatibility with many ecommerce platforms. They accept the majority of payment types.

Supply Chain Management Systems (SCM)

Supply Chain management systems are essential for maintaining a healthy stockflow and for maintaining a good relationship with both your suppliers and the people you supply. Many businesses have gone under for no other reason than bad managment of their stock.

Oracle SCM


oracle.com
Oracle is a robust SCM, it manages the supply chain of both current and indevelopment products as well as inventory management, supply sourcing and delivery. Their feature list is large but includes

  • Warehouse logistics
  • Product lifecycle management
  • Supply chain planning
  • Order managment
